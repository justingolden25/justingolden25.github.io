Custom LED Ceiling —
Delta Chi Chapter Room

A fully custom addressable LED matrix designed and engineered from scratch — 24V DC power distribution, ESP32 + WLED control, level-shifted signal routing, and a clean professional installation above a T-bar drop ceiling.

August 2025 — April 2026 Electrical Engineer & Designer Georgia Tech, Atlanta GA

01 / Overview

Project Overview

The Custom LED Ceiling is a fully self-directed electrical engineering build: designing and installing a custom addressable LED lighting system in the Delta Chi chapter room at Georgia Tech. Six lanes of addressable LED strips — three on each side of the ceiling — total approximately 1,000 individually addressable pixels, with all power and control electronics mounted above the T-bar drop ceiling tiles and completely out of view.

The core engineering challenge was running a low-voltage addressable LED system above a drop ceiling cleanly and reliably — managing voltage drop over long cable runs to six parallel lanes, maintaining signal integrity across every lane simultaneously, providing independent overcurrent protection per lane, and keeping every wire and component invisible from the room below. The goal was a system that works reliably and looks like it belongs there.

02 / Technical Deep Dive

Engineering Details

Section A

System Architecture

  • 6 lanes of WS2812B-compatible addressable LED strips — 3 per side of the ceiling
  • 24V DC power supply feeds a centralized fuse block with one fuse per lane for independent fault protection
  • 24V architecture reduces current draw and I²R losses over long cable runs compared to 5V or 12V systems
  • Buck converter (24V → 5V) powers the ESP32 and level-shifter logic from the same main supply
  • Structured grounding throughout — all grounds bonded at a single point to prevent ground loops

Section B

Perfboard Control Circuit

  • Custom perfboard consolidates the ESP32 and level shifter on a single board with a shared 5V power and ground bus bar
  • IC sockets for both ICs allow either component to be swapped without desoldering — designed for easy field troubleshooting
  • ESP32 outputs 3.3V WLED data; the level shifter steps this up to 5V for LED strip compatibility
  • 6 level-shifter outputs — one per lane — each with a dedicated 330Ω series resistor soldered to the board to damp signal reflections at the strip inputs
  • Each output connects via an individual data wire running to its corresponding LED lane

Section C

Control System

  • ESP32 microcontroller running WLED open-source firmware
  • Fully Wi-Fi controlled via the WLED web app from any phone or laptop on the local network
  • ~1,000 individually addressable pixels driven as a single continuous segment across all six lanes
  • Supports a wide range of effects: color waves, strobes, chases, solid fills, color palettes, and custom presets
  • All control hardware mounted above the ceiling — no visible equipment in the room

Section D

Diffusion & Mounting

  • Silicone LED diffuser channels zip-tied directly to the T-bar drop ceiling rails — no drilling, no permanent modification to the space
  • Clear zip ties used throughout for a clean, minimal look
  • All wiring routed above the ceiling tiles — no exposed cables visible from the room below
  • Diffusers soften individual LED hotspots into a smooth, even glow along the full length of each lane
